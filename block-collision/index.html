Elastic Collision Simulation

At time t = 0, Block A slides towards Block B. After the collision, Block B's velocity is updated. Watch the blocks collide!

Solution:

1. Apply the elastic collision formula to calculate the final velocity of Block B after the collision:

v2 = ((m1 - m2) * u1 + 2 * m2 * u2) / (m1 + m2)

Where:
m1 = 6 kg (mass of Block A)
m2 = 2 kg (mass of Block B)
u1 = 2 m/s (initial velocity of Block A)
u2 = 0 m/s (initial velocity of Block B)

2. After applying the formula, you will get the final velocity of Block B: 0 m/s

3. This represents the velocity of Block B immediately after the collision.
